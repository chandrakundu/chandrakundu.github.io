Research

Research Overview

My research lies at the intersection of Mathematics and Artificial Intelligence. I develop efficient, theoretically grounded algorithms augmented by machine learning and neural networks for analyzing high-dimensional data that are often noisy, incomplete or corrupted. These methods have broad impact across hyperspectral imaging, video surveillance, biomedical data, and Internet of Things (IoT) systems.

Robust Matrix Completion

A core area of my work is Robust Matrix Completion, an approach designed to simultaneously handle incomplete data and sparse outliers. I have developed algorithms such as Robust CUR Completion (RCURC) and Learned Robust Matrix Completion (LRMC), a deep learning-augmented framework that demonstrate strong performance across multiple domains:

  • Video Surveillance: Video background subtraction.
  • Imaging: Ultrasound imaging and face modeling.
  • Remote Sensing: Cloud removal from multi-temporal satellite imagery.

Distance Geometry Problems

I also focus on Distance Geometry problems, which involve finding the point configurations from the distances measured among those points. I’ve developed algorithms like Structured Robust Euclidean Distance Geometry (SREDG) and Robust Euclidean Distance Geometry via Dual Basis (RoDEoDB), which show strong results in critical applications:

  • Localization: Sensor localization.
  • Bioinformatics: Protein structure prediction (molecular conformation).

Weighted Matrix Completion

Weighted Matrix Completion (WMC) is another key area of my research, aimed at incorporating prior subspace information to improve recovery performance. The work centers on compressive hyperspectral imaging in pushbroom remote sensing. Hyperspectral data are captured as a 3D cube of spatial pixels $(x, y)$ and spectral channels $(z)$, as illustrated below. The key challenge is to reconstruct high-dimensional data slices $D$ in real time from only a small, incomplete set of observed entries $b$ (see Figure 2).

We are currently working on developing a novel WMC framework that will use prior knowledge from the previously reconstructed slice to recover the current slice. Crucially, we use machine learning to automatically predict the optimal weight for each slice, ensuring the fastest, most accurate real-time streaming reconstruction for efficient data processing.

Collaborative Research

As a Data Scientist, my expertise extends to interdisciplinary research where I apply data-driven methods to challenges beyond core algorithms. I have worked with colleagues at the Nanoscience and Technology Center, UCF, applying machine learning to predict the discharge capacity of $\text{MnO}_2$ cathodes for high-performance zinc-ion batteries. I have also contributed to digital humanities, using statistical techniques for authorship attribution. I am eager to continue pursuing research that bridges disciplines and leverages my strengths in data science.
